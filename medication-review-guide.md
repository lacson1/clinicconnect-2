# Medication Review System Guide

## How to Perform Medication Reviews

### Overview
The medication review system allows healthcare professionals to systematically review patient medications for safety, efficacy, and appropriate usage.

### Accessing Medication Reviews

1. **Navigate to Patient Records**
   - Go to the Patients section
   - Select a patient from the list
   - Click on the patient's name to open their detailed profile

2. **Find the Medication Review Tab**
   - In the patient's profile, look for the "Medication Review" tab
   - This tab is typically located alongside other clinical tabs like "Visits", "Lab Results", etc.

### Creating a New Medication Review Assignment

1. **Click "Assign Review" Button**
   - In the Medication Review tab, click the blue "Assign Review" button
   - This opens the assignment creation dialog

2. **Fill Out Assignment Details**
   - **Review Type**: Select from options like:
     - General Medication Review
     - Drug Interaction Check
     - Dosage Optimization
     - Side Effect Assessment
     - Compliance Review
   
   - **Assign To**: Choose a healthcare professional (pharmacist, doctor, nurse)
   - **Priority**: Set as Low, Medium, High, or Critical
   - **Due Date**: Set when the review should be completed
   - **Notes**: Add any specific instructions or concerns

3. **Submit the Assignment**
   - Click "Create Assignment" to save

### Performing a Medication Review

1. **Access Assigned Reviews**
   - Healthcare professionals can see their assigned reviews in:
     - The patient's Medication Review tab
     - Their personal dashboard under "Pending Reviews"

2. **Start the Review Process**
   - Click "Start Review" on a pending assignment
   - This changes the status from "Pending" to "In Progress"

3. **Review Components**
   - **Current Medications**: Review all active prescriptions
   - **Drug Interactions**: Check for potential conflicts
   - **Dosage Appropriateness**: Verify correct dosing
   - **Patient History**: Consider allergies and previous reactions
   - **Lab Results**: Check if medications affect lab values

4. **Document Findings**
   - Add detailed notes about your review
   - Flag any concerns or recommendations
   - Suggest medication changes if needed

5. **Complete the Review**
   - Click "Complete" when finished
   - Add final recommendations and notes

### Review Types Explained

**General Medication Review**
- Comprehensive review of all medications
- Check for appropriateness and necessity
- Identify potential discontinuations

**Drug Interaction Check**
- Focus on medication interactions
- Review new medications against existing ones
- Check for contraindications

**Dosage Optimization**
- Review current dosages for effectiveness
- Consider patient's age, weight, kidney function
- Adjust for optimal therapeutic effect

**Side Effect Assessment**
- Evaluate patient complaints of side effects
- Determine if symptoms are medication-related
- Recommend alternatives if needed

**Compliance Review**
- Assess patient adherence to medication regimen
- Identify barriers to compliance
- Suggest simplification strategies

### Status Tracking

- **Pending**: Review has been assigned but not started
- **In Progress**: Review is currently being performed
- **Completed**: Review has been finished with recommendations
- **Cancelled**: Review was cancelled for some reason

### Best Practices

1. **Thorough Documentation**
   - Always document your findings clearly
   - Include specific recommendations
   - Note any follow-up actions needed

2. **Patient Communication**
   - Discuss findings with the patient when appropriate
   - Explain medication changes clearly
   - Provide written instructions

3. **Collaboration**
   - Communicate with prescribing physicians
   - Involve other healthcare team members
   - Share critical findings promptly

4. **Follow-up**
   - Schedule follow-up reviews as needed
   - Monitor patient response to changes
   - Track medication effectiveness

### Troubleshooting

**If you can't see medication reviews:**
- Ensure you have the appropriate role permissions
- Check that you're logged in with the correct account
- Verify the patient has active prescriptions

**If assignments aren't showing:**
- Refresh the page
- Check your role permissions
- Contact your system administrator

**For technical issues:**
- Try logging out and back in
- Clear your browser cache
- Contact technical support
